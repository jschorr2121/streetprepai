# Interview Prep — Product Description

> A functional, non-technical description of the product. For advisors, early users, partners, and future hires.

---

## What it is

An AI-powered interview preparation platform for college students recruiting into investment banking. It replaces the patchwork of PDFs, forums, and spreadsheets students use today with a single place to read, practice, network, and track progress. AI doesn't do the work for you — it reads alongside you, drills you on what you just read, remembers every conversation you have, and shows you exactly what to work on next.

## Who it's for

- **Phase 1 (day one):** US undergraduate sophomores and juniors targeting Investment Banking Summer Analyst roles.
- **Close after that:** non-target undergrads who need more structure and networking support, and MBA / career switchers entering banking.
- **Future:** adjacent finance recruiting — Private Equity, Equity Research, Sales & Trading, Corporate Development, and internationally (London, Hong Kong).

## The problem

IB recruiting is an uneven playing field where information, prep, and relationships decide who gets the offer. Students today face:

- **A maze of resources** — WSO (forum), Wall Street Prep and BIWS (video courses), Mergers & Inquisitions (blog), Handshake (jobs), LinkedIn (networking), Excel modeling tests scattered across Dropbox links. Nothing connects.
- **Passive content** — mostly static PDFs and video lectures. Students read without engaging, forget what they read, and can't tell what they actually know.
- **No memory of networking** — after 40 coffee chats over a semester, students forget who said what. They write generic follow-ups, miss the perfect hook for an interview, and let warm connections go cold.
- **No honest signal** — no one tells them what they actually need to work on. Mock interviews are with friends who are just as uncertain.
- **Seasonal pressure, year-round uncertainty** — IB recruiting accelerates earlier every year; students who aren't organized by sophomore summer get left behind.

## How it works — the recruiting journey, with this product

1. **Onboard.** Student tells us their school, year, target firms, and what they already know. Dashboard calibrates: here's what you're ready for, here's what you're not.
2. **Read actively.** Open a guide on DCF. Any paragraph you highlight pops an AI bubble in the side rail that explains it in plain English — or you ask a follow-up. A "Beginner Mode" toggle has the AI rewrite each section at your level with analogies, while the canonical expert-level text stays one click away. Section-by-section summaries ("lenses") adjust to your goal: *I'm prepping for GS TMT Superday Tuesday.*
3. **Chat with the content.** Ask the guide itself: "Walk me through a DCF as if you were explaining it to an interviewer." The AI answers with citations back to the sections you just read.
4. **Frame your experiences.** Type a rough description of your summer internship or leadership role. The AI produces five interview-ready framings — a STAR leadership story, a STAR teamwork story, a conflict story, a resume bullet in banker-speak, a "why banking" hook — each editable, saved to your Story Bank.
5. **Practice out loud.** In the Mock Interview Studio, pick a mode (technical, behavioral, firm-specific, or a full Superday rotation). Record your answer by voice. Seconds later, you get a scorecard with a content score (was the answer accurate and structured?) and a delivery score (pace, filler words, length) — plus realistic follow-up questions and a model answer to compare against.
6. **Network with memory.** Every coffee chat, interview, or recruiter call lives in your Relationship Hub. Before each one, the AI generates a prep sheet — for a person: their background, coverage area, smart questions to ask, personal hooks; for a firm: latest earnings, recent deals, league-table position, and any notes from chats *you've already had* at that firm. After each chat, three-sentence notes become a structured summary, auto-drafted follow-up email, and a scheduled reminder to reconnect. When you get an interview invite, your own past conversations at that firm resurface automatically.
7. **See what you need to work on.** The Progress page is not about trophies — it's a weak-area diagnostic. Mock scores, flashcard accuracy, quiz results, and time spent reading all feed a model that tells you: here are the top three topics to drill, and here's the fastest way to do it.
8. **Find the jobs.** Job Hub aggregates open IB roles across firms — filter by firm, group, region, graduation year, and deadline. One-click apply links take you directly to the firm's ATS.
9. **Ask for human help.** Book a 30-minute session with a current analyst or associate at a target firm. Optional, paid, revenue-shared with the mentor.
10. **Repeat, with compounding.** Every interaction makes the next one smarter — your Story Bank, your mastery map, your relationship memory grow over the cycle.

## Features, in user-facing language

### Content Library
Structured guides covering: Accounting, Valuation (DCF, Comps, Precedents), M&A, LBO, Restructuring, Behavioral & Fit, Firm Guides (BB, EB, MM, with group-level intel), Networking, Resume & Cover Letter, Modeling Tests, Superday logistics, Market & Deal News.

### Active Reading Lens
Highlight any passage → AI bubble explains it and lets you ask follow-up questions about that exact passage. Beginner Mode rewrites the content at your level. Section summaries ("lenses") adapt to your interview goal. Highlights become flashcards. Notes stay in the margin.

### Chat with Content
Every guide has an AI chat scoped to its content, with clickable citations back to the source sections.

### Mock Interview Studio
Voice-based interview practice. Whisper transcribes, Claude scores content and delivery, gives follow-up questions and a model answer. Modes: Technical, Behavioral, Firm-specific, full Superday rotation.

### Behavioral Story Framer
Input a raw experience → receive multiple STAR-format framings tailored to each behavioral angle, plus a resume bullet and a "why banking" hook. Saved framings feed the Mock Interview Studio.

### Progress & Weak-Area Diagnostics
A dashboard that tells you what you need to work on, not just what you've done. Mastery heatmap by topic, ranked weak areas with one-click drills, spaced-repetition queue weighted toward your gaps, streak calendar, weekly review digest.

### Job Hub
Aggregated IB roles with rich filters. Save roles, track application status, get deadline alerts.

### Resume & Deal Sheet Coach
Upload resume → AI rewrites bullets in banker-speak, flags weak items, suggests framings. Deal sheet builder for returning interns.

### Networking & Alumni
Find mentors by firm and group. Book sessions. Templates and scripts for cold outreach and coffee chats.

### Relationship Memory & Prep Hub
A CRM plus AI memory layer for your entire recruiting funnel. Syncs with your calendar. Tracks every contact, every chat, every outreach email. Pre-generates prep sheets for both coffee chats (about the person) and interviews (about the firm, including earnings and recent deals). Structures your post-chat notes. Drafts personalized follow-ups. Surfaces relevant past conversations when you interview at a firm. Gentle nudges to reconnect when it matters. All personal notes encrypted, never used for training, fully exportable and deletable.

### Community
Peer forum for interview reports, study groups, and mock-partner matching. Moderated, invite-only to start.

### Office Hours & Events (later)
Scheduled AMAs and recorded sessions with practicing bankers.

## Why now

Three things just became true at the same time:

1. **LLMs are good enough to actually explain finance concepts at a student's level, score interview answers, and remember long, messy relationships** — something that was science fiction 18 months ago.
2. **IB recruiting timelines compressed by a full year** — sophomores are now prepping for SA interviews that used to be a junior-year concern. The market desperately needs tools that shorten the path from "clueless" to "ready."
3. **The incumbents are old software** — WSO was built in 2006, BIWS in 2009. Students live in Notion, Linear, Superhuman, and Granola. A modern, AI-native tool for this audience is overdue.

## Pricing (intended)

- **Free tier:** limited guides, 3 mock interviews per month, job feed.
- **Pro Monthly:** ~$29/mo — full library, unlimited mocks, resume coach, all lenses, progress tracking.
- **Recruiting Season Pass:** ~$149 one-time, August through February. Most students don't subscribe — they buy the cycle.
- **Mentor sessions:** $40–120 per session, paid to mentors (revenue-share).
- **University Plan:** licensed to career centers, free for enrolled students.

## The promise

*You stay in the work. The AI stays beside you.* We don't read for you, we don't write your answers, we don't fake your story. We lift friction, remember what you'd forget, and show you exactly where you're weak. You do the thinking. You get the offer.
