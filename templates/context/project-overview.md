# Street Prep AI

## Overview

Street Prep AI is the unified preparation platform for US undergraduates targeting investment banking Summer Analyst roles. It combines a structured, course-like **learning flow** — chapters covering everything from application strategy and firm overviews to sector deep-dives, networking, technicals, behavioral interviews, and superday prep — with a layered set of **AI-powered tools** that go where the learning leaves off: a tool-using chatbot that can pull from the user's profile, networking history, and live firm data; a resume coach; a behavioral story framer; voice mock interviews plus HireVue-style video practice with AI-graded scorecards; a technical question bank; and a relationship manager with calendar integration that remembers every coffee chat. The learning flow doesn't just teach concepts — it teaches users how to use the platform's own tools in context (the Networking chapter walks them through Relationship Manager; the Behavioral chapter walks them through the Story Framer). Every existing IB-prep resource (WSP, BIWS, WSO, M&I, Handshake) covers one slice; Street Prep AI unifies the entire recruiting cycle with AI lifting friction at every step.

## Goals

1. Provide an authenticated, secure experience where every feature is accessible to signed-in users.
2. Deliver an intuitive, informative product that prepares users end-to-end for IB recruiting.
3. Guide users through a structured learning flow whose chapters are weighted toward technical mastery (accounting, EV vs equity value, valuation, DCF, M&A, LBO, brain teasers) — alongside the surrounding chapters on cycle/timeline, applications, firm and sector intel, resume, networking, behavioral, mock interviews, and superday. Each chapter combines reading-heavy guides, interactive tutorials (inline exercises, worked examples, AI socratic tutoring — mixed per chapter), and AI-graded practice questions.
4. Let users complete AI-graded practice technical questions from a curated bank or AI-generated by topic, with difficulty levels (easy / medium / hard), follow-up question trees that mimic real interviewer probing, and spaced re-surfacing of weak questions every 2–3 days.
5. Let users do voice mock interviews and HireVue-style video practice, scored by AI on both content and delivery, with adaptive follow-up questions that mirror real interviewer behavior.
6. Let users upload a resume and receive AI recommendations to improve it; the same upload populates their profile.
7. Give users a tool-using AI chatbot that answers any IB recruiting question by accessing their profile, experiences, networking history, the web, general IB knowledge, and firm data — including "prep me for why JPM"-style synthesis pulling from everything the user has done.
8. Let users generate behavioral story framings from their real experiences using the Story Framer.
9. Let users track and semantically search every networking call and coffee chat, and discover new people worth reaching out to.
10. Provide firm-specific pages with live earnings, deals, intel, and 10–15 firm-specific interview questions per firm; and sector deep-dives by industry, supporting both the learning flow and the chatbot.
11. Integrate Google Calendar so coffee chats and interviews auto-link to contacts and trigger pre-event prep sheets.
12. Track user progress via a mastery model (weak-area diagnostics) layered with completion statistics and an activity streak.
13. Surface a personalized **recruiting cycle widget** on the dashboard, anchored to the user's grad year and current semester, so users always know where they are in the timeline and what to focus on this semester.

## Core User Flow

1. **Sign up and authenticate.** Email or Google OAuth.
2. **Onboard.** Upload resume → AI parses it into a structured profile (experiences, school, grad year, current semester). Pick target firms.
3. **Land on the dashboard.** Surfaces a **recruiting cycle widget** ("You're in Sophomore Fall — BB applications open in 4 months. Focus areas this semester: networking + technical foundations"), position in the learning flow, recommended next chapter, upcoming calendar events with prep sheets ready, top 3 weak areas, completion stats, and streak.
4. **Work through the learning flow.** Chapters in sequence: Recruiting Cycle & Timeline → How to Apply → Firm Overviews → Sector Deep-Dives → Resume & Cover Letter → Networking Mastery → Behavioral & Fit → Technicals (Accounting → EV vs Equity Value → Valuation: Comps & Precedents → DCF → M&A → LBO → Brain Teasers & Mental Math) → Mock Interviews & HireVue Practice → Superday & Logistics. Inside each chapter: reading (with Reading Lens for inline AI explanation of any highlighted passage), one or more interactive tutorials (the author picks the format — inline exercises, worked examples, or AI socratic tutoring), and AI-graded practice questions.
5. **Chapters teach the tools in context.** The Networking chapter walks the user through how to network properly, also shows the Relationship Managerto=. The Behavioral chapter walks them through the Story Framer. The Resume chapter uses Resume Coach. The Mock Interview chapter uses the Mock Interview Studio + HireVue practice. Pedagogy and practice are integrated.
6. **Open a tool at any time.** Chatbot, Story Framer, Resume Coach, Mock Interview / HireVue, Technical Question Bank, Relationship Manager — all accessible from the sidebar.
7. **Before any networking event or interview** (auto-detected via Google Calendar): AI-generated prep sheet using the user's profile, past chats with anyone at the same firm, the firm's live page (earnings + deals + intel), and relevant learning content.
8. **After events:** log notes → AI structures them into a typed summary → drafts a warm, specific follow-up email → schedules a future check-in reminder.
9. **Ask the chatbot anything.** "Prep me for why JPM." "How should I study for LBOs?" "Who did I talk to about Apollo's culture?" The chatbot routes to its tools (profile, networking history, web, IB knowledge, firm data) and synthesizes.
10. **Progress compounds.** The mastery model updates from every chapter completion, practice answer, mock score, and tutorial response, surfacing weak areas with one-click drill CTAs alongside completion stats and streak.

## Features

### Learning Flow (the spine)

A structured, course-like progression of chapters. Each chapter combines:

- **Reading** built around the **Reading Lens** — highlight any passage to get an instant plain-English AI explanation in the side rail; **Beginner Mode** rewrites a section in accessible language with analogies.
- **Interactive tutorials** — the author picks the format per chapter from: inline guided exercises ("calculate EBITDA from this income statement"), worked-example walkthroughs (step-by-step reveals), or AI socratic tutoring (the AI quizzes the user conversationally before they move on).
- **AI-graded practice questions** at the end of each section.

Chapter sequence (16 chapters; technicals are 7 of them — the bulk of the curriculum, with reading-heavy guides and graded practice):

- **Recruiting Cycle & Timeline** — the IB recruiting cycle, what to be doing each semester from freshman through junior year, BB vs EB vs MM, group types (M&A, LevFin, ECM/DCM, sector groups), applications-open dates, rolling-review reality.
- **How to Apply** — application mechanics, where to apply, target-firm count by tier, ATS/GPA filters, cover letters when required.
- **Firm Overviews** — bulge brackets (GS, MS, JPM, BofA, Citi, Barclays, UBS, DB), elite boutiques (Evercore, Lazard, Centerview, Moelis, PJT, Guggenheim, Rothschild), middle markets (Jefferies, Houlihan Lokey, Harris Williams, William Blair, Baird, RBC), and group-level intel where notable.
- **Sector Deep-Dives** — TMT, healthcare, FIG, energy, consumer & retail, industrials, real estate, financial sponsors.
- **Resume & Cover Letter** — one-page conventions, banker-bullet templates (action verb + what + quantified result), deal sheet structure, ATS-friendly formatting, common red flags; teaches and exercises **Resume Coach**.
- **Networking Mastery** — finding bankers (LinkedIn alumni search, finance clubs, career center), cold-email anatomy and templates, coffee-chat prep and conduct, follow-up cadence; teaches and exercises **Relationship Manager**.
- **Behavioral & Fit** — STAR structure, building a 5–8 story bank covering leadership/failure/conflict/teamwork/persuasion/ethics/why-IB, walk-me-through-your-resume narrative, firm-specific behavioral prep ("why GS / JPM / MS"); teaches and exercises **Story Framer**.
- **Technicals — Accounting & 3 Statements** — income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement, how the three link, working capital, deferred items, lease accounting.
- **Technicals — Enterprise Value vs Equity Value** — formulas, the bridge, EV-items vs equity-items, why EV/EBITDA but not Equity/EBITDA, classic edge cases (issuing $50M debt and holding as cash).
- **Technicals — Valuation: Comps & Precedent Transactions** — building comp sets, choosing multiples (EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, EV/EBIT, P/E), precedent transactions and the control premium, valuation hierarchy.
- **Technicals — DCF** — full DCF mechanics from FCF projection to terminal value (Gordon Growth and Exit Multiple methods) to WACC. The single most-tested topic; deserves its own chapter.
- **Technicals — M&A & Merger Models** — accretion/dilution, synergies (revenue and cost), stock vs cash deals, goodwill creation, purchase price allocation, full merger-model walkthrough.
- **Technicals — LBO Models** — sources & uses, operating model, debt schedule, returns analysis (IRR, MOIC), what makes a good LBO candidate, dividend recaps.
- **Technicals — Brain Teasers & Mental Math** — common brain teasers, mental math drills (multiplication, percentages, IRR estimation), how to think out loud under pressure.
- **Mock Interviews & HireVue Practice** — interview formats (HireVue, first round, Superday), what scorers look for on content vs delivery, day-of mechanics; teaches and exercises **Mock Interview Studio** and **HireVue practice**.
- **Superday & Logistics** — what to expect across a full day of 5–7 interviews with analysts/associates/VPs/MDs, behavioral consistency, energy management, thank-you cadence.

### AI Tools (available at any time)

- **AI Chatbot.** Tool-using assistant. Tools: profile lookup, networking history search (semantic), web search, IB knowledge base, firm data. Handles "prep me for why JPM" by synthesizing across the user's experiences, prior chats at JPM, JPM's live firm page, and general IB context.
- **Behavioral Story Framer.** User pastes a raw experience → Claude generates multiple STAR framings (leadership, teamwork, conflict, failure, analytical) plus a walk-me-through-resume bullet and a why-banking hook. Saves to a personal Story Bank tagged by angle. Surfaces gaps ("no conflict story yet").
- **Resume Coach.** Upload PDF → AI flags weak bullets, rewrites in banker-speak, side-by-side diff. Same upload populates the user's structured profile.
- **Mock Interview Studio + HireVue Practice.** Voice-based mocks (Whisper transcription) and HireVue-style video practice. AI scorecards include: content score (rubric breakdown with citations back to relevant chapters), delivery score (words-per-minute, filler-word count, pause ratio, on-camera presence for HireVue), **adaptive follow-up questions** that mirror real interviewer probing (each answer can branch into 3–5 follow-ups), and a model answer. Trends tracked over time.
- **Technical Question Bank.** User picks a topic and difficulty (easy / medium / hard) → system serves a question from a curated database or AI-generates a fresh one. AI grades the user's answer against the rubric. Each question has a **follow-up question tree** of 3–5 deeper probes that fire when the user answers correctly. **Spaced re-surfacing**: weak/incorrect questions automatically re-appear every 2–3 days until mastered.
- **Relationship Manager.** Contacts list + Google Calendar OAuth (auto-detects coffee chats and interviews + auto-links events to contacts). **Pre-event prep sheets**: one click on a contact gathers everything (your profile + their LinkedIn background + their firm's recent earnings/deals + your past chats at that firm) and produces a prep brief with what to know and questions to ask, calibrated to their seniority (analyst / associate / VP / MD). Same flow for firm-side interview prep. Post-event note logging → AI structures into a typed summary (topics, advice, commitments, follow-up items, personal details). Follow-up email drafter referencing the specific conversation. Semantic search across all chats ("who did I talk to about Apollo's culture"). **Discover new people to network with** (specific mechanism deferred to a later spec round).

### Profile & Progress

- **Profile.** Populated from the resume upload (experiences, school, grad year, current semester, prior roles). Editable. Holds target firms. Read by the chatbot, story framer, prep sheets, mock-interview question selection, and the recruiting cycle widget.
- **Progress Tracking.** Hybrid model:
  - **Mastery model** under the hood — Bayesian/weighted score per topic from practice answers, mock scores, time-on-section, chapter completion. Surfaces "top 3 gaps right now" with one-click drill CTAs. Drives the Q-bank's spaced re-surfacing.
  - **Completion stats** in the UI — chapters complete, practice questions answered, mocks taken.
  - **Activity streak** — daily-use signal, surfaced honestly (no dark patterns).
- **Recruiting Cycle Widget.** Dashboard surface anchored to the user's grad year + current semester. Tells the user where they are in the cycle ("Sophomore Fall — BB applications open in 4 months"), what to focus on this semester (networking, technical foundations, etc.), and what's coming next. Personalizes the dashboard's recommendations.

### Firms & Sectors

- **Firm Pages.** Per-firm reference page: recent earnings summary (synthesized from SEC filings), recent announced deals, league table position, recent news, **firm-specific interview question set** (10–15 questions known to be asked at that firm, sourced from interview reports), culture notes pulled from the user's own past chats at that firm. Cached and refreshed weekly. Used by the learning flow's Firm Overviews chapter, by pre-interview prep sheets, and by the chatbot.
- **Sector Pages.** Per-sector reference: coverage area, recent deals, top firms in the sector, key terminology. Used by the learning flow's Sector Deep-Dives chapter and by the chatbot.

### Auth & Account

- Authenticated experience required for all features. Sign in via email or Google OAuth. Profile, progress, contacts, story bank, and chats are all per-user with row-level security.

## Scope

### In Scope

- Authenticated experience for all features (Supabase auth).
- Resume upload → AI parsing → structured profile (incl. school, grad year, current semester, target firms).
- Full learning flow: 16 chapters combining reading, interactive tutorials, and AI-graded practice. Technicals weighted as the bulk (7 of 16 chapters).
- Reading Lens (highlight → AI explain) and Beginner Mode (AI rewrite of a section).
- All six AI tools: Chatbot (with profile / networking / web / IB knowledge / firm-data tools), Story Framer, Resume Coach, Mock Interview Studio + HireVue practice, Technical Question Bank, Relationship Manager.
- Technical Question Bank with difficulty levels (easy / medium / hard), follow-up question trees (3–5 deeper probes per Q), and spaced re-surfacing of weak Qs every 2–3 days.
- Mock Interview Studio with adaptive follow-up questions on each answer.
- Firm pages with live earnings/deals/intel + 10–15 firm-specific interview questions per firm, refreshed weekly.
- Sector deep-dive pages.
- Google Calendar OAuth with auto-linking of events to contacts and pre-event prep sheets.
- Mastery model + completion stats + activity streak.
- Recruiting cycle widget on the dashboard, personalized to grad year and current semester.
- Discover new people to network with (mechanism deferred — feature is in scope, implementation approach to be decided in a later spec round).

### Out of Scope

- Job hub / job board / job scraping (cut from current codebase scope).
- Community forum, peer discussion, interview-report exchange.
- Mentor marketplace, paid 1:1 mentor sessions.
- Office hours, live AMA events with practitioners.
- Spaced-repetition flashcards as a standalone feature.
- Other finance verticals (PE, ER, S&T, corp dev, consulting) — explicitly deferred to "much later."
- International tracks (London, Hong Kong) — explicitly deferred.
- Native mobile app — out of phase 1.
- Payment processing, paid tiers, subscription management — out of phase 1; focus first on building the product.

## Success Criteria

1. A new user can sign up, upload a resume, and see their profile populated with experiences, school, grad year, and target firms.
2. A signed-in user can complete a learning flow chapter end-to-end: read the section using Reading Lens for inline AI explanations, finish at least one interactive tutorial, and answer practice questions that the AI grades with rubric feedback.
3. A user can ask the chatbot "prep me for why JPM" and receive a personalized synthesis that pulls from (a) their profile, (b) past chats with anyone at JPM, (c) the JPM firm page's latest earnings and deals, and (d) general IB knowledge — visibly citing each source.
4. A user can take a voice mock interview *or* a HireVue-style video mock and receive an AI-graded scorecard containing content score (with rubric and chapter citations), delivery score (with measurable signals), follow-up questions, and a model answer.
5. A user can paste a raw experience into the Story Framer and receive multiple STAR framings (leadership, teamwork, conflict, failure, analytical) plus a resume bullet and why-banking hook, saved to their Story Bank.
6. A user can upload their resume to Resume Coach and see weak bullets flagged with AI-rewritten suggestions in banker-speak via a side-by-side diff.
7. A user can pick a topic and difficulty (easy / medium / hard) in the Technical Question Bank, receive a question (curated or AI-generated) that the AI grades after they answer, and trigger a follow-up question when they answer correctly. Weak/incorrect questions automatically resurface 2–3 days later.
8. A user can add a contact, log a chat about that contact, and receive an AI-drafted follow-up email that references the actual conversation specifics (not a generic thank-you).
9. A user can connect Google Calendar; coffee chats and interviews appear auto-linked to contacts on the dashboard, with pre-event prep sheets generated and ready to read.
10. A user can semantically search their networking history ("who did I talk to about Apollo's culture?") and the right past chat is surfaced as the top result.
11. A user can browse firm pages and see live earnings/deals/intel plus 10–15 firm-specific interview questions, and sector pages by industry.
12. A user's progress dashboard shows top 3 weak areas (from the mastery model) with drill CTAs, alongside completion statistics and current streak.
13. A user's dashboard shows a recruiting cycle widget reflecting their grad year and current semester, with concrete focus recommendations for that semester ("BB applications open in 4 months — focus on networking and technical foundations").
14. A user takes a mock interview, answers the question, and the AI fires an adaptive follow-up question that mirrors how a real interviewer would probe deeper based on what they said.
